YOGA

What is yoga?

It is difficult to find a person in the world today who has never heard of yoga. This is the most mysterious teaching and this is what makes it interesting. Translated from Sanskrit, “yoga” means “connection”, “fusion”, “connection”. The question immediately arises - merging, with what or with whom? In short, yoga classes involve the merging of the source of all that exists with its manifestations in a person and the disclosure of his soul.


For millions of people, yoga has become a unique way to find inner harmony, improve health, get rid of psychological problems, forget about stress and bad mood forever, learn to enjoy every passing day, i.e. become truly happy.


Originating in India several millennia ago, today yoga has changed dramatically. Many schools and directions have appeared that have turned a huge culture into ordinary physical education. But the physical improvement of a person is only a small part of yoga, and far from being the main one. The yoga system has many components, these are asanas (movements), pranayamas (breathing practices), shatkarmas (cleansing techniques), mantras, meditation, proper nutrition and much more.

What is meditation and why is it needed?

A modern person not only communicates a lot with other people, but also constantly conducts an internal dialogue with himself. Endless thoughts that do not stop for a second in our head do not allow us to relax and disconnect from problems, they become the cause of mental disorders.


Meditation helps to stop the internal monologue with oneself. A person, by an effort of will, stops mental activity and the silence of the mind begins. Any yoga practice begins with this, to disconnect from everything and completely immerse yourself in what you are doing at the moment - doing asanas, breathing exercises, cleansing, or just sitting in a comfortable position. At this time, you open your soul, penetrate into the depths of consciousness, free yourself from everything that has accumulated inside and that interferes with life.
